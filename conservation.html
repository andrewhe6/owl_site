Conservation

Currently, it is estimated that only about 800 Pueo owls are left in the wild, leading scientists to declare it as an endangered species. Because Pueos like to dwell on the ground, they are vulnerable to cats, rats, and mongoose. They are also sometimes seen to suffer from "SOS" or Sick Owl Syndrome, which seems to cause them to walk onto roads, resulting in an increased mortality rate in Kaua'i. There is no known cause of SOS but scientists believe it could be due to pesticides while others believe it is caused by seizures from light pollution. Other threats to the Pueo include hunting and loss of habitat from deforestation and construction.
